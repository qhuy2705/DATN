## Design Context

### Users
PrimeCare serves three primary groups:

- Public patients and caregivers who need to discover services, compare doctors, book appointments, and look up results from phones or laptops, often while anxious or short on time.
- Returning patients using the self-service portal to review appointments, results, invoices, and profile settings without relying on phone support.
- Internal clinic staff and clinicians including reception, service desk, doctors, cashier, pharmacy, and administrators who need fast, dependable workflows during busy operating hours.

The core job to be done is to make healthcare coordination feel clear, reliable, and low-friction from discovery through booking, visit, follow-up, and internal operations. The product is bilingual and aligned to Vietnam-based healthcare workflows, so the experience should support Vietnamese-first practical clarity with strong English parity.

### Brand Personality
PrimeCare should feel trustworthy, calm, and precise.

The interface should project clinical competence without feeling cold. Public-facing screens should reduce anxiety, create reassurance, and make next steps obvious. Internal tools should feel orderly, efficient, and controlled, helping staff move quickly with confidence. The voice should remain respectful, plainspoken, and supportive rather than promotional, playful, or overly technical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light-first healthcare aesthetic built around a blue-and-white medical palette. The visual language should feel clean, modern, calm, and accessible: crisp white surfaces, softly blue-tinted neutrals, a deep medical blue for navigation and primary actions, and restrained secondary accents only when they improve comprehension.

The overall atmosphere should read as clinical daylight and organized care, not startup flash. Favor generous whitespace, strong typography, clear content grouping, and subtle motion. Forms, tables, timelines, and patient summaries should feel dependable and legible before they feel decorative. Rounded corners and shadows should stay disciplined and understated. Avoid visual noise, heavy gradients, neon accents, glass effects, luxury styling, or futuristic AI aesthetics. PrimeCare should not look like a fintech dashboard, a pastel wellness app, or a dark-mode tech product.

### Design Principles
1. Reassure first. Every screen should lower anxiety by making status, next steps, and important information immediately legible.
2. Clarity over cleverness. Use simple hierarchy, direct language, and layouts that support fast scanning on both mobile public pages and denser internal workflows.
3. Trust through restraint. Use measured blue-white surfaces, precise spacing, and minimal decoration so the product feels credible, hygienic, and professionally maintained.
4. Accessibility is the baseline. Maintain strong contrast, visible focus states, keyboard support, reduced-motion respect, generous tap targets, and unambiguous validation and error feedback.
5. One system, multiple contexts. Keep a cohesive brand across public, patient, and staff surfaces while adapting density, copy, and interaction patterns to each audience and to both Vietnamese and English content.
